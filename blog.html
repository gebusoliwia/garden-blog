Tips for Pruning Roses

Roses have a reputation of being difficult to growâ€”as long as you feed them and prune them correctly, you shouldn't have too much trouble with these pretty plants. Pruning is a vital element of rose plant care and the longevity of the plant in your garden.

Rose Pruning Principles:

1. Always prune dead wood back to healthy tissue.
2. After you make each cut, cover it with a drop of white glue.
3. Prune to ensure the center of the bush is open for maximum air circulation.

How to Prune Roses:

1. Make your pruning cuts at a 45-degree angle, about 1/4 inch above a leaf axle with a dormant eye.
2. Choose an eye on the outside of the cane and slope the cut down and away on the opposite side.
3. If the rose bush has foliage present, the location for your cut is easy to spot.

How to Make Compost

Some common misconceptions of home composting are that it's too complicated, it'll smell funny, and it's messy. These are all true if you compost the wrong way. Luckily, composting the right way is quite simple: Just layer organic materials and a dash of soil to create a concoction that turns into humus (the best soil booster around!).

What to Compost:

1. Fruit scraps.
2. Coffee grounds.
3. Grass and plant clippings.

What NOT to Compost:

1. Anything containing meat, oil, fat, or grease.
2. Diseased plant materials.
3. Dog or cat feces.

How to Grow Citrus Trees Indoors

For me, there's nothing more enchanting than the perfume of citrus blossoms in late winter. Growing citrus trees indoors can be daunting, but the effort is well worth it for the aromatic flowers and foliage, not to mention the much-anticipated fruit.

Best Pots for Indoor Citrus Trees:

1. Always make sure the container you do use has a drainage hole.
2. Don't use a pot that's too big.
3. Mix of citrus potting soil ($20, Etsy) and orchid bark (from $6, Etsy).

Best Types of Citrus for Growing Indoors:

1. Thai lime.
2. Finger lime.
3. Meyer lemon.
